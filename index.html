The EARTH

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. It is the densest planet in the Solar System and the largest of the four terrestrial planets. Earth’s atmosphere consists mostly of nitrogen and oxygen. Earth’s axis of rotation is tilted with respect to its orbital plane, producing seasons on Earth1

The MOON

The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite. Its diameter is about one-quarter of Earth’s (comparable to the width of Australia), making it the fifth largest satellite in the Solar System and the largest and most massive relative to its parent planet. It is larger than all known dwarf planets in the Solar System

The VENUS

The temperature on Venus is extremely hot. The average surface temperature is about **864 degrees Fahrenheit (462 degrees Celsius)**. That's hotter than the surface of Mercury, the planet closest to the Sun! The high temperature is due to a runaway greenhouse effect caused by the thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide.

The MERCURY

During the day, temperatures on the surface can reach 800 degrees Fahrenheit (430 degrees Celsius). Because the planet has no atmosphere to retain that heat, nighttime temperatures on the surface can drop to minus 290 degrees

The SUN

The temperature of the sun varies from around 27 million degrees Fahrenheit (15 million degrees Celsius) at the core to only about 10,000 degrees F (5,500 degrees C) at the surface1. The Sun has a temperature ranging from 6,000° Celsius at its surface which is hot enough to turn gold, tungsten and every metal on Earth into gas

The MARS

On average, the temperature on Mars is about minus 80 degrees F (minus 60 degrees Celsius) according to NASA. In winter, near the poles, temperatures can get down to minus 195 degrees F (minus 125 degrees C)

The JUPITER

From an average distance of 484 million miles (778 million kilometers), Jupiter is 5.2 astronomical units away from the Sun. With an average temperature of minus 234 degrees Fahrenheit (minus 145 degrees Celsius), Jupiter is frigid even in its warmest weather

The SATURN

Saturn is nine and a half times farther from the sun than Earth, with an average distance of 886 million miles (1.4 billion kilometers) On the surface, Saturn’s average temperature varies from about minus 185 degrees Celsius (minus 300 degrees Fahrenheit) to minus 122 C (minus 188 F).

The URANUS

The distance between Earth and Uranus changes daily because the solar system is in constant motion. The closest the two get is 1.6 billion miles (2.6 billion kilometers). On the surface, Uranus' temperature is **minus 197 degrees Celsius (minus 322 degrees Fahrenheit)**. I hope that helps!

The NEPTUNE

When Neptune is closest to our planet, it is at a distance of 2.7 billion miles. When it is at its farthest from Earth, 2.8 billion miles lie between the two planets. The temperature at Neptune’s “surface” is approximately minus 346 F (minus 201 C). The planet’s average temperature is minus 353 F (minus 214 C)
